Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common queries regarding revenue services and county permits.

What is the deadline for paying annual Land Rates?

Annual land rates are due on or before March 31st of every calendar year. Outstanding land rates after this date attract penalty interest at 1% per month compounded, in accordance with the Meru County Rating Act.

How is the Single Business Permit (SBP) fee determined?

The permit fee is calculated based on: (1) The type of business activity (Retail, Transport, Hospitality, Agriculture, etc.), (2) The size of the business (number of staff, floor area), and (3) The location zone (Urban, Peri-Urban, or Rural). You can estimate your SBP cost using the Fee Calculator on this website.

Is cash accepted at Mt Kenya House offices?

No, the Meru County Government is 100% cashless. Cash payments at bank counters or county offices are not accepted. All payments should be made using M-Pesa Paybill 440112, direct bank deposits to designated county accounts, or online through our self-service portal.

How do I get a payment receipt?

Once you complete your payment on the MeruPay portal (either by M-Pesa STK or card), the system automatically processes your payment status. A digital receipt will appear instantly on screen, which you can print or download. Additionally, a confirmation SMS will be sent to the phone number on file.

How do I register a brand-new business in Meru County?

To register a new business, you will need: (1) A valid KRA PIN certificate, (2) A Certificate of Business Registration or Incorporation from the Registrar of Companies, (3) A copy of the proprietor/director's National ID, and (4) Proof of business premises (lease agreement or title deed). Bring these to the MCRB offices at Mt Kenya House or visit our portal to initiate the process digitally. A county officer will then conduct a physical inspection before the permit is issued.

What is agricultural produce cess, and who is required to pay it?

Agricultural cess is a levy charged on designated farm produce (including Miraa/Khat, Tea, Coffee, Avocado, and other crops) when those goods are transported out of or through Meru County. It is collected at designated county weighbridges and border checkpoints. The cess is payable by the transporter or owner of the produce, computed based on vehicle tonnage and the applicable rates in the Meru County Finance Act. All payments are cashless at collection points.

Can I get a penalty waiver on my land rates or permit arrears?

The County Government periodically announces penalty and interest waiver programmes for land rates and permit arrears. These windows are usually communicated through official county notices, the Kenya Gazette, and on this website. During a waiver period, you pay only the principal dues — all accumulated interest and penalties are cancelled. Watch the Announcements section on this page for current waiver windows. Outside waiver periods, penalties continue to accrue at 1% per month for land rates.

What do I do if my payment went through on M-Pesa but my bill still shows outstanding?

First, keep your M-Pesa confirmation SMS safe — it is your proof of payment. Then contact our support desk at info@merurevenue.go.ke or visit us at Mt Kenya House with the following: your M-Pesa transaction code, the exact amount paid, the date of payment, and the bill reference number you used. Most reconciliation issues are resolved within 2–3 working days. You can also submit an enquiry directly through the Contact page on this site.

Overview

About Meru County Revenue Board

The Meru County Revenue Board (MCRB) is the official revenue administration body of the County Government of Meru, established under the Meru County Revenue Board Act, 2014. Our mandate is to oversee, assess, collect, and account for all charges, levies, rates, and fees imposed by the County Government of Meru in a fair, efficient, and transparent manner.

We are the people behind the county you love. Every road that gets repaired after the rains. Every market that has running water. Every public school that gets new desks. Every health facility that stays open — these things happen because someone paid their dues. And someone else made sure those dues were collected, accounted for, and put to work.

That someone is us. And we take that responsibility seriously.

Our Vision

To be a model of excellence in automated local revenue administration, facilitating a prosperous and self-sustaining Meru County.

Our Mission

To maximize revenue collection through customer-centric service, innovative technology, transparent administration, and absolute integrity.

Our Journey

The Story Behind the Numbers

When devolution came to Kenya, it brought a promise — that communities would have more say over their own development. The Revenue Board was created to make that promise real.

2013

Established by Law

The Meru County Finance Act 2013 laid the framework for an independent revenue body. MCRB was born out of a belief that counties could — and should — fund their own growth.

2019

First Major Milestone

Annual collections reached KES 540 million — a 69% increase from the previous year. Dedicated enforcement teams, better officer training, and growing public trust made the difference.

2020

Going Digital

MCRB partnered with JamboPay to automate major revenue streams. For the first time, residents could pay parking fees, market dues, SBPs, and land rates without setting foot in a county office.

2023

Cashless by Default

Cash payments were officially phased out across all 24 revenue streams. Real-time monitoring, a new USSD shortcode (*412#), and automated billing slashed leakages and doubled enforcement effectiveness.

2025

Breaking the Billion Barrier

Meru County collected KES 1.15 Billion in own-source revenue. The next target: KES 2 Billion — and beyond that, unlocking the county's full estimated potential of KES 3.7 Billion.

Directives

Core Mandates & Functions

The operational directives of the MCRB are structured to drive transparency, compliance, and public accountability.

1. Revenue Collection & Administration

Assessing, collecting, and accounting for all county revenues. Automation of revenue payment systems has greatly reduced leakages by enabling real-time monitoring of transactions, collections, and deployment of field personnel.

2. Enforcement of Revenue Collection

Deploying dedicated revenue and enforcement officers across all sub-counties, towns, and border points to drive compliance. The enforcement strategy has successfully doubled revenue streams at key weighbridges and produce cess stations.

3. Digitisation & Cashless Integration

Transitioning county systems to a 100% cashless flow. Business licenses, daily parking, agricultural cess, municipal market dues, advertisements, and rental surcharges are paid via mobile money, card, and USSD, cutting transaction times and leaks.

4. Land Rates & Valuation Rolls

Imposing and collecting land rates on rateable property. MCRB is working to update the Meru County Valuation Roll in partnership with FAO to resolve collection bottlenecks, recover outstanding dues, and tap into millions of potential property revenues.

5. Reporting & Financial Accountability

Submitting standard monthly and annual performance reports to the County Governor and the County Assembly, ensuring full alignment with county budgets and compliance with the Public Finance Management Act.
